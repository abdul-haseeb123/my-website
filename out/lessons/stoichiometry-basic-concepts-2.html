# Stoichiometry: Basic Concepts in Chemistry **1. Introduction** * Chemistry: The study of the properties, composition, structure, and transformations of matter. * Atom: The smallest unit of an element, retaining all the chemical characteristics unique to that element. Examples: Ne, Fe, Na, Ca * Molecule: A molecule consists of two or more atoms chemically bonded together. Examples: H2O, CH4, NH3, HCl * Ion: An ion is an atom or molecule that possesses a net electric charge. Ions can be categorized into two types: cations, which carry a positive charge, and anions, which carry a negative charge. These charges result from the loss or gain of electrons, respectively. * Positively charged ions: Na⁺, H⁺, Ca²⁺, N³⁺, Al³⁺ etc. * Negatively charged ions: C⁴⁻, C²⁻, O²⁻, S²⁻ etc. * Formula units: In chemistry, the formula units describe the simplest whole-number ratio of ions in an ionic compound. Example: In the case of common table salt, the formula unit is NaCl. This indicates that for every sodium ion (Na⁺), there is one chloride ion (Cl⁻) in the compound. * Element: An element represents the purest and simplest form of matter, comprising atoms with identical atomic numbers and cannot be chemically decomposed further. Examples: Ne, Fe, Na, Ca, He, Au, Ag * Compound: A compound is a pure substance composed of atoms or ions from two or more different elements in specific proportions and can be chemically broken down into simpler substances. Examples: H2O, H2O2, CH4, NH3, HCl, NaCl, FeCl3, Na2CO3 etc. **2. Atomic Number and Mass** * Atomic Number (Z): The atomic number is the count of protons in an element's nucleus, often represented as 'Z.' * Mass Number (A): The mass number is the sum of protons and neutrons in an atom's nucleus, typically denoted as 'A,' with the equation A = Z + N. * Atomic Mass (Atomic Weight): Atomic mass, also known as atomic weight, refers to the average mass of all isotopes of an element, compared to the mass of one atom of carbon-12 (amu). * Atomic mass = Σ (mass of isotope i × abundance of isotope i) * Atomic Mass Unit (amu): An atomic mass unit, denoted as amu, is a unit of mass used to express the atomic or molecular weight of substances. It is defined as 1/12 of the mass of a carbon-12 atom. * 1 amu = 1.66054 × 10⁻²⁷ kg * 1 amu ≈ 9.11 × 10⁻³¹ kg **3. Chemical Formulas** * A chemical formula is a symbolic representation used to describe a compound. * Example: Water: H₂O, Lime stone OR Calcium carbonate: CaCO₃ **Types of Formulas:** 1. **Empirical Formula (Simplest Formula):** The empirical formula, or simplest formula, represents the smallest or most basic ratio of atoms within a compound. It is less commonly used in chemical reactions. Many ionic compounds, which consist of non-discrete ions, can be represented by the empirical formula. * Examples: * Sodium Chloride (NaCl) * Magnesium Chloride (MgCl₂) 2. **Molecular Formula:** The molecular formula shows the actual number of atoms present in a compound and is frequently used in chemical reactions. Most molecular compounds, which consist of discrete molecules, can be represented by both the empirical and molecular formulas. * Examples: * Benzene: C₆H₆ (Molecular Formula) * Glucose: C₆H₁₂O₆ (Molecular Formula) * The molecular formula can be determined using the formula: * Molecular Formula = (Empirical Formula) n * Where; * n is a whole number, calculated as n = mass of molecule / mass of empirical formula **4.
